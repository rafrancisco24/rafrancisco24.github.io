SIATON

Siaton, Negros Oriental, is known as the "rice bowl of the south" in Negros. It is a coastal municipality located in the southern tip of Negros Island, Philippines, and is often referred to as the southermost settlement in the province.

Discover the Beauty of Siaton, Negros Oriental

Siaton, Negros Oriental, is a first-class municipality known for its natural beauty and vibrant culture. Nestled at the southernmost part of Negros Island, it is home to fertile rice fields, scenic coastal areas, and the famous Lake Balanan, surrounded by rolling hills and forests. Visitors can explore pristine beaches, mountains, and forests, while enjoying local festivals and traditions that showcase the town’s agricultural and fishing heritage. Siaton’s friendly community, combined with its eco-tourism attractions, makes it a perfect destination for adventure seekers and travelers looking for a serene, authentic experience.

Explore the Best of Nature, Culture, and Adventure

Balanan Lake

Balanan Lake is a tranquil natural destination in Siaton surrounded by forested hills, offering kayaking, swimming, and peaceful scenic views for nature lovers.

Turtle Island

Turtle Island is a small, picturesque islet off the coast of Siaton, perfect for snorkeling, beach relaxation, and spotting marine life in crystal-clear waters.

Tambobo Bay

Tambobo Bay is a serene coastal haven in Siaton known for its calm waters, yacht anchorage, and relaxing seaside views perfect for boating and sunset escapes.

Giligaon Falls

Giligaon Falls is a hidden gem in Siaton that features a refreshing cascade, a natural pool, and lush forest surroundings perfect for swimming and nature adventures.

Events and Festival

Where Traditions Dance and Colors Come Alive!

Siaton Fiesta

The Siaton Fiesta, celebrated in honor of St. Nicholas of Bari, is a vibrant gathering that brings together families, visitors, and the community in joyful thanksgiving. The town comes alive with colorful processions, lively cultural performances, and heartfelt religious traditions that reflect the faith and unity of the people of Siaton. Beyond the festivities, the celebration highlights the town’s rich heritage and warm hospitality. It is a time of renewal, gratitude, and pride for all Siatonians.

Inagta Festival

The Inagta Festival of Siaton celebrates the rich folklore of the Agta, forest spirits believed to protect nature and the community. Through vibrant dances, dramatic face and body paint, and rhythmic beats, performers reenact the harmony between humans and the natural world. The festival highlights Siaton’s deep connection to its environment and its commitment to preserving cultural heritage. It is a joyful showcase of identity, tradition, and local pride.
